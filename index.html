Case Facts & Arguments

Plaintiff

Royal Lepage Team Realty Inc

  • Seeking the 5% commision payment.
  • “Dual agency” which means that they have to act in the best interest of the seller and buyer.
  • Were at awe with the house condition and stated that the house was worth between $338,000 and $340,000.
  • Mary Lindsay states that if they want a quick sale, it should not be listed above $330,000.
  • Third party agent offered $302,000 and Mary Lindsay encouraged them to seriously consider the offer, which the plaintiff rejected.
  • Another third party agent had a "serious offer" of $315,000, which the plaintiffs were not interested in.
  • Paul Williams, the husband of Kelly Williams who worked in the same office as the Lindsays, had an offer.
  • Wrote on the offer that “The buyer, Paul Williams, is the spouse of Kelly Williams, the Royal Lepage Team Realty sales representative”.
  • Did not use the name "Kelly Ellard" in the call with Karen. The court agrees that it may be due to the stress affecting Karen and Dan that they might have been confused.
  • Overstepped authority and made an unapproved counter offer of $325,000 which was ratified by Karen and Dan.

Defendant

Karen Temple & Daniel Donovan

  • Bought a house in the City of Ottawa in November 2002, with renovations approximating to $100,000.
  • Daniel gets a job at Newmarket, Ontario in 2005 and wants to quickly sell the house.
  • Hires Mary and David Lindsays as their real estate agents and signs a Multiple Listing Agreement.
  • Karen and Dan stated that Mary Lindsays referred to the agent as "Kelly Ellard."
  • Daniel Donovan has degrees in economics and history from University of Ottawa and the University of Strasbourg in France with a prestigious work experience.
  • Karen and Dan asked if the Lindsays knew Kelly Williams and Mary stated that she had "heard of her" but was not sure if she had met her.
  • Karen and Dan had a "heated" discussion on the price to attempt to obtain the house at $329,500 rather than $320,000.
  • Although did not authorize an offer of $325,000, Karen and Dan signed an agreement of purchase and sale to sell the house to Paul Williams.

Case Timeline

  • November 1, 2002 Karen and Dan acquired their house in Ottawa for the price of $255,000.
  • August 8, 2005 The Lindsays attended the home and found it to be in superior condition and highly saleable.
  • August 10, 2005 Karen and Dan entered into a Agreement with the Plaintiff Royal Lepage Team Realty for the price of $329,500.
  • August 12, 2005 An offer was made for $302,000. They refused and countered after which the offer expired.
  • August 14, 2005 An open house was held to show the home which was attended by several visitors.
  • August 15, 2005 An offer was made for $315,000, but Karen and Dan were not interested in accepting $315,000.
  • August 18, 2005 An agents open house was held to show the home.
  • August 22, 2005 Karen and Dan agreed to sell the house at $325,000 to Paul Williams.
